1. Introduction

There are three types for buttons: flat buttons, square buttons, and round buttons.

  • Flat buttons are text-only buttons. They may be used in dialogs, toolbars or inline. They fill with color on mouseover
  • Square buttons are rectangular-shaped buttons. They are the most commonly used buttons. They have a background color on idle state, and they have a different background color on mouseover.
  • Round buttons are circular, icon-only buttons. They are used for ubiquitous and important actions. They can be further categorised into two sub-types:
    • Ubiquitous: They have a thick border and a white background on idle state. They are used for some commonly understood actions, which no text descriptions is needed.
    • Important: They have a thick border and a colored background on idle state. They are used for some important quick actions. And they attract peopleâ€™s attention by their shape and background colors.
2. Choosing button style

Choosing a button style depends on the primacy of the button, the number of containers on screen, and the screen layout.

  • Function: Is it important and ubiquitous enough to be a Round button?
  • Dimension: Choose Square or Flat button depending on the container it will be in and how many layers you have on screen. There should not be many layers of objects on the screen
  • Layout: User one type of buttons per container as much as possible. Only mix button types when you have a good reason to, such as emphasizing an important function.
3. Style
3.1 Typography

Button text should be capitalized, and use the same font with the body

3.2 Dimension

Square buttons and flat buttons should have a minimum height of 24px

Round buttons should have a minimum height of 32px

3.3 Corner radius
  • Square buttons should have a 2px corner radius.
  • Round buttons should simply have a 100% corner radius.
4. Buttons
4.1 Flat buttons

Flat buttons are text-only buttons, which do not have any background or icon. They should be used in the following locations:

  • Toolbar
  • Dialogs
  • Inline with margin and padding, so the user know they are buttons

However, they may still have some background color when they are not in idle state.

4.2 Square buttons

Square buttons are more standout than flat buttons. So, they can be used to differentiate between page content and functions.

Square buttons have similar behavior as flat buttons when they are not idle.

4.3 Dropdown buttons

Dropdown buttons are similar as square buttons, but with a down arrow to indicate they are dropdown buttons. Please find 3.6_Menus for more information about dropdowns.

4.4 Group buttons

Group buttons, also known as toggle buttons, may be used to group related options. They have more states than normal buttons, such as on and off. And multiple buttons can be on or off at the same time.
